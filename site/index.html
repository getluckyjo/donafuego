The 60-second case

The catalyst. The launch is signed, not hoped for: 360 Checkers stores plus Sixty60 from August 2026, cleared through multiple tasting panels, with an opening order of roughly R3 million — the biggest purchase order in the distillery's history.

The gap. South Africa is the world's third-largest RTD market, and roughly 95% of its spirit-based RTDs are vodka. There is no credible grab-and-go premium margarita on the shelf. Doña Fuego is real imported tequila and real juice at 7% ABV — built for exactly that space.

The operator. Doña Distillery has run since 2019 on one playbook: build a brand, ship it, repeat. Its brands sell locally and internationally, and it imports the tequila inside competing RTD products. Doña Fuego is the next brand off a working production line.

The deal. The investment is the brands — Doña Fuego cans and the nationally selling Fuego Tequila bottles — secured by a dedicated supply agreement with the distillery. Projected can revenue of R13.2m at 30% gross margin by December 2027, before a single bottle is counted.

Why Checkers

South Africa's fastest-growing grocer five years running — a near-R100 billion brand with 311 LiquorShops and Sixty60 delivery growing 47.7% a year. A 360-store exclusive covers essentially its entire premium footprint, at the premium end of the shelf: R49 a can against R39–42 mainstream, justified by the liquid, not the label.

The exclusivity is the strategy. Twelve months inside SA's best retailer to prove rate of sale — then a demonstrated listing story goes to the ±2,400 branded liquor doors watching: TOPS at SPAR (914 stores), Pick n Pay Liquor (~459), Liquor City (~300). Checkers is the launchpad, not the ceiling.

The business

Two brands. One supply lock.

The investment is in the tequila brands — with bulk tequila secured by a special supply agreement with the distillery behind them.

Brand 1 — Doña Fuego cans

Margarita, Spicy Margarita, Paloma and a Non-Alcoholic Margarita. 250ml cans, 7% ABV, R169 a 4-pack, exclusive to Checkers. Projected R13.2m revenue at 30% gross margin through December 2027, off the Checkers listing alone.

Brand 2 — Fuego Tequila bottles

Reposado, jalapeño-infused and cocktail serves — already trading nationally, from the Oranjezicht Market to major retailers. In the deal; not yet in the projections.

The founder

The operator behind the bet

Danielle Schoeman has already done the hard version of this. Stellenbosch business degree, then self-taught distilling in the Karoo after tasting real tequila in Mexico; founded Doña Distillery in 2019 and took national trophies for Best Ready-to-Drink and Best Low/No-Alcohol Drink by year two. Food & Home named her South Africa's youngest female master distiller.

She has built, shipped and sold spirit brands before — Doña Fuego is that playbook, applied to the category she started with. Production runs under a dedicated manager; her hands are on sales, retail and brand.
