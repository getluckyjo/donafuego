03 · Product & brand

Premium, and priced like it

The range. Margarita, Spicy Margarita, Paloma, Non-Alc Margarita — 250ml cans, 7% ABV (rivals sit at 4.5–5%). On the label: "Made with real tequila, lemon & lime juice. No BS."

The price. R169 per 4-pack / R49 a can — the premium end of the shelf against R39–42 rivals, earned with real imported tequila, real juice and higher ABV.

The bottles. Fuego Tequila — Reposado, jalapeño-infused and cocktail serves — already selling nationwide, from the Oranjezicht Market to major retailers. A premium anchor above the cans, fully in the deal.

The positioning. The anti-wine club: competing for share of the cooler box — the braai, the boat, the beach house — aimed at urbanites who already order margaritas out.

04 · The market

Three tailwinds, one open shelf

3rd

SA is the world's third-largest RTD market, volumes +12% in 2024 (IWSR).

of SA's spirit RTDs are vodka-based (TSB). Tequila RTD is open white space.

2.4×

SA tequila: $129m (2024) → $309m by 2033, 9.2%/yr (IMARC).

10.5%

Global tequila CAGR — $18bn → $45bn by 2033. The fastest-growing major spirit (IMARC).

The momentum is spirit RTDs

Spirit RTDs were the only growing US spirits category in 2025: +16.4% (DISCUS); tequila and canned cocktails were called the year's only bright spots (CNBC). SA's spirit-RTD segment is projected to more than double to ~$2.4bn by 2031 (Mobility Foresights).

The input-cost window

Mexico's agave price has crashed ~90% from peak — panic sales as low as 1–2 pesos/kg — with a ~500-million-litre tequila surplus looking for buyers (France24, Robb Report). For an importer of bulk tequila, it's the best buying window in a decade — protecting RTD margins and strengthening the bulk arm's economics.

The retail partner: Checkers

SA's fastest-growing grocer five years running — sales +13.8% to a near-R100bn brand, 311 LiquorShops, Sixty60 delivery +47.7% (Shoprite FY2025). With ~660 stores in total, a 300-store exclusive covers essentially its entire premium footprint.

One caveat, managed head-on: US tequila dollar sales dipped 4.1% in 2025 as the category traded down — the same softness that created the glut this business buys into. SA's tequila and RTD markets both remain in structural growth.

06 · Go-to-market

Launch deep, not wide

The listing does the reach

300 Checkers stores from July 2026 with a 12-month exclusive. Consumer campaign live September 2026. In a listed launch, every store is a billboard — the marketing job is rate of sale, not awareness.

Own one occasion

"Share of cooler box." Target existing margarita drinkers — no money spent converting anyone. Goal: 1,000 repeat customers in the first six months (~2 four-packs/month), found within 100km before going wide.

Partner-led campaign

Premium cooler-bar partnership with in-store giveaways at top stores, surprise cooler-box drops, and a #DoñaFuego photo competition that doubles as audience research. No price discounting — the premium holds.

10 · Investor FAQ

Asked and answered

This list grows as investors ask. If your question isn't here, ask it — it'll help the next reader.

What exactly am I investing in?
The tequila business only: the Doña Fuego brand (trademark, SKUs, the Checkers listing and future retail relationships), the Fuego Tequila bottled range, plus the bulk tequila import-and-supply operations. It excludes the distillery's other brands (Mahala Botanical, Toor Whisky) and general contract distilling. The intended structure is a dedicated brand entity with a supply agreement back to the distillery.
Are the Fuego Tequila bottles included?
Yes. The bottled range — 750ml Reposado, jalapeño-infused tequila and cocktail serves — is part of the tequila business being invested in, and is already selling nationwide, from the Oranjezicht Market to major retailers. Bottle revenue comes over and above the cans-and-bulk numbers in the model.
Where does the ~R20m valuation come from?
An independent triangulation of three methods on the model's 2027 figures: EV/EBITDA (6–12×), EV/Revenue (1–2.5×) and a simplified DCF (WACC 15–22%). The blended range is R19.5m–R38.5m; the ~R20m guide deliberately sits at the bottom of that range. Full workings are in the downloadable model's Valuation sheet.
What is the money actually used for?
First, the working-capital gap of the Checkers ramp — tequila, cans and SARS excise are paid before Checkers' 30-day terms pay out, creating a peak funding need of R1.56m in August 2026. Beyond that: deeper stock cover for reorders, the September 2026 consumer campaign, and opportunistic bulk tequila purchases while Mexican prices are at decade lows.
Isn't the tequila boom cooling?
In the US, dollar sales dipped 4.1% in 2025 as the category traded down — while volumes held and canned cocktails kept growing. That softness is exactly what crashed agave and bulk-liquid prices, which is a buy-side gift for this business. South Africa's tequila market (forecast +9.2%/yr to 2033) and RTD market (world's third largest) are both still in structural growth.
What happens when the Checkers exclusivity ends?
The 12-month exclusive is the launch vehicle, not the ceiling. After it lapses (mid-2027), the brand can open other national retailers, on-trade and export — none of which is in the current projections. The model assumes only the initial 300 stores at a flat 600-case monthly reorder.
Who else is on the SA tequila RTD shelf?
A handful of young brands (see section 05) — and several of them, including Patch and Cheeky, buy their bulk tequila from Doña. Doña Fuego holds the premium slot: real imported tequila, real juice, 7% ABV versus the field's 4.5–5%.
Why is the RTD gross margin 30% and not higher?
Because it's honest: it carries the full 20% SARS excise line, packaging inflation and co-pack fees. Bulk supply adds a second 28%-margin stream with customers paying 75% upfront, which is what keeps the combined EBITDA margin at ~28% with minimal overhead.
Is there a non-alcoholic angle?
Yes — the fourth SKU is a Non-Alcoholic Margarita, made by a distillery that won a national trophy for Best Low/No-Alcohol Drink and already runs a non-alc brand (Mahala Botanical) sold in the UK. No-and-low is the fastest-growing shelf in the store.
