01 · Executive summary

The whole pitch, in one page

Doña Fuego is a tequila RTD brand with a national retail listing already signed. Four SKUs — Margarita, Spicy Margarita, Paloma and a Non-Alcoholic Margarita — made with real imported Mexican tequila and real juice, launching July 2026 exclusive to 300 Checkers stores, with an opening order of roughly R3 million (2,700 cases), the biggest PO in the distillery's history.

Behind the brand sits a proven supply business. Doña Distillery has imported bulk Mexican tequila for years and sells it to competing RTD brands — several of the tequila RTDs on SA shelves run on Doña's liquid. Bulk customers pay 75% upfront, which finances the working-capital cycle.

The numbers: R20.97m projected revenue from June 2026 to December 2027, split almost evenly between the Checkers RTD business (R10.29m at 30% gross margin) and bulk supply (R10.68m at 28%). EBITDA of R5.89m at a 28.1% margin. Peak funding requirement of R1.56m in August 2026; the business is projected cash-positive from November 2026 and ends 2027 with R5.13m cash.

The ask: one strategic partner — capital plus capability — at a valuation guided by an independent triangulation of ~R20m (blended range R19.5m–R38.5m). The investment covers the tequila business only: the Doña Fuego cans, the Fuego Tequila bottled range with its direct-to-consumer store, and bulk tequila sales.

02 · Company & scope of the investment

What you're investing in

Doña Distillery (Pty) Ltd — founded 2019, Cape Town, 100% female-owned. The distillery produces its own portfolio (Mahala Botanical non-alcoholic spirits, Toor Whisky with a partner, Doña Fuego Tequila), contract-distills for other brands, exports to Europe and North America, and imports bulk Mexican tequila at scale. Its custom vacuum stills are the only ones in Africa.

Scope of this investment: the tequila business only. That means the Doña Fuego brand (trademark, product range, the Checkers listing and future retail relationships), the Fuego Tequila bottled range — a 750ml Reposado, a jalapeño-infused tequila and cocktail serves, with its direct-to-consumer store at fuegotequila.co.za — plus the bulk tequila import-and-supply operations. It excludes Mahala Botanical, Toor Whisky and the general contract-distilling business.

  • Intended structure: the tequila business is being packaged into a dedicated brand entity holding the Doña Fuego trademark, with a supply agreement back to the distillery for production.
  • Why now: the raise is timed on the Checkers listing and the growth story — before a short trading history anchors the valuation to early actuals.
  • Preference: a strategic partner over passive equity — someone who adds distribution, brand-building or retail muscle.
  • Final legal structure, instrument and terms to be agreed with the incoming partner and confirmed in due diligence.
03 · Product & brand

Premium, and priced like it

The range: Margarita, Spicy Margarita, Paloma, Non-Alcoholic Margarita. 250ml slim cans at 7% ABV (most competitors sit at 4.5–5%), classified "Spirit Cooler" on-pack. Label promise: "Made with real tequila, lemon & lime juice. No BS."

Pricing: R169 per 4-pack / R49 per can at Checkers — deliberately the premium end of the shelf against R39–42 mainstream competitors. The premium is earned: real imported tequila (not "tequila flavour"), real juice, higher ABV.

The bottles: the same imported tequila is sold under the Fuego Tequila label — 750ml Reposado, jalapeño-infused, and bottled cocktail serves — direct to consumers at fuegotequila.co.za. The bottled range is part of this investment and gives the brand a premium anchor above the cans.

Positioning: the anti-wine club. Doña Fuego competes for share of the cooler box — the braai, the boat, the beach house — as the drink you bring when everyone else brings wine. Launch persona: cosmopolitan late-20s/30s urbanites who already drink margaritas at restaurants and want the grab-and-go version without buying a bottle and a cocktail kit.

04 · The market

Three tailwinds, one open shelf

1 — South Africa is a global RTD heavyweight

South Africa is the world's third-largest RTD market, up from fifth, with RTD volumes growing 12% in 2024 (IWSR). RTDs are already the second-biggest alcoholic drinks segment in the country — and roughly 95% of SA's spirit-based RTDs are vodka-based (The Spirits Business). Tequila-based RTD is effectively open white space. The SA RTD spirits segment is projected to more than double from ~$1.1bn (2025) to ~$2.4bn by 2031 (Mobility Foresights).

2 — Tequila is the world's growth spirit

Global tequila was worth $18.4bn in 2024 and is forecast to reach $45.4bn by 2033 (10.5% CAGR — IMARC). In the US it became the second-most-valuable spirits category, and in 2025 tequila and canned cocktails were called the only bright spots in a soft spirits market (CNBC). Spirit RTDs were the only growing US spirits category in 2025: +16.4% (DISCUS). At home, the SA tequila market is forecast to grow from $128.5m (2024) to $308.8m by 2033 — 9.2% a year (IMARC).

3 — A once-in-a-decade input-cost window

Mexico's agave price has collapsed from ~35 pesos/kg at the peak to low single digits — panic sales as low as 1–2 pesos/kg — after growers surged from 3,100 to 42,000+ (France24, AgriBrasilis). Mexico is sitting on a ~500-million-litre tequila surplus (Robb Report). For a business whose raw material is bulk Mexican tequila, this is the best buying window in a decade — it protects RTD margins and makes the bulk-supply arm's buy-side economics unusually strong.

The retail partner: Checkers

Checkers is South Africa's fastest-growing grocer five years running — sales up 13.8% to a near-R100bn brand, with 311 LiquorShops and Sixty60 delivery up 47.7% to R18.9bn (Shoprite Holdings FY2025). Checkers operates roughly 660 stores in total, so a 300-store exclusive covers essentially its entire premium footprint — and Checkers has a long playbook of exclusive premium liquor listings (e.g. Private Barrel Co.) used as traffic drivers.

One caveat, managed head-on: US tequila dollar sales dipped 4.1% in 2025 as the category traded down. That same softness created the agave and bulk-liquid glut this business buys into — and SA's tequila and RTD markets both remain in structural growth.

06 · Go-to-market

Launch deep, not wide

The listing does the reach

300 Checkers stores from July 2026 with a 12-month exclusive. Consumer campaign live September 2026. In a listed launch, every store is a billboard — the marketing job is rate of sale, not awareness.

Own one occasion

"Share of cooler box." Target existing margarita drinkers — no money spent converting anyone. Goal: 1,000 repeat customers in the first six months (~2 four-packs/month), found within 100km before going wide.

Partner-led campaign

Premium cooler-bar partnership with in-store giveaways at top stores, surprise cooler-box drops, and a #DoñaFuego photo competition that doubles as audience research. No price discounting — the premium holds.

10 · Investor FAQ

Asked and answered

This list grows as investors ask. If your question isn't here, ask it — it'll help the next reader.

What exactly am I investing in?
The tequila business only: the Doña Fuego brand (trademark, SKUs, the Checkers listing and future retail relationships), the Fuego Tequila bottled range with its direct-to-consumer store (fuegotequila.co.za), plus the bulk tequila import-and-supply operations. It excludes the distillery's other brands (Mahala Botanical, Toor Whisky) and general contract distilling. The intended structure is a dedicated brand entity with a supply agreement back to the distillery.
Are the Fuego Tequila bottles included?
Yes. The bottled range — 750ml Reposado, jalapeño-infused tequila and cocktail serves — and the fuegotequila.co.za online store are part of the tequila business being invested in. Note that bottle and DTC revenue is not included in the financial projections, which cover only the Checkers RTD listing and bulk supply — the bottles are additional upside on the same numbers.
Where does the ~R20m valuation come from?
An independent triangulation of three methods on the model's 2027 figures: EV/EBITDA (6–12×), EV/Revenue (1–2.5×) and a simplified DCF (WACC 15–22%). The blended range is R19.5m–R38.5m; the ~R20m guide deliberately sits at the bottom of that range. Full workings are in the downloadable model's Valuation sheet.
What is the money actually used for?
First, the working-capital gap of the Checkers ramp — tequila, cans and SARS excise are paid before Checkers' 30-day terms pay out, creating a peak funding need of R1.56m in August 2026. Beyond that: deeper stock cover for reorders, the September 2026 consumer campaign, and opportunistic bulk tequila purchases while Mexican prices are at decade lows.
Isn't the tequila boom cooling?
In the US, dollar sales dipped 4.1% in 2025 as the category traded down — while volumes held and canned cocktails kept growing. That softness is exactly what crashed agave and bulk-liquid prices, which is a buy-side gift for this business. South Africa's tequila market (forecast +9.2%/yr to 2033) and RTD market (world's third largest) are both still in structural growth.
What happens when the Checkers exclusivity ends?
The 12-month exclusive is the launch vehicle, not the ceiling. After it lapses (mid-2027), the brand can open other national retailers, on-trade and export — none of which is in the current projections. The model assumes only the initial 300 stores at a flat 600-case monthly reorder.
Who else is on the SA tequila RTD shelf?
A handful of young brands (see section 05) — and several of them, including Patch and Cheeky, buy their bulk tequila from Doña. Doña Fuego holds the premium slot: real imported tequila, real juice, 7% ABV versus the field's 4.5–5%.
Why is the RTD gross margin 30% and not higher?
Because it's honest: it carries the full 20% SARS excise line, packaging inflation and co-pack fees. Bulk supply adds a second 28%-margin stream with customers paying 75% upfront, which is what keeps the combined EBITDA margin at ~28% with minimal overhead.
Is there a non-alcoholic angle?
Yes — the fourth SKU is a Non-Alcoholic Margarita, made by a distillery that won a national trophy for Best Low/No-Alcohol Drink and already runs a non-alc brand (Mahala Botanical) sold in the UK. No-and-low is the fastest-growing shelf in the store.
