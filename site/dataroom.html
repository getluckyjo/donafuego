03 · Product & brand

Premium, and priced like it

The range. Margarita, Spicy Margarita, Paloma, Non-Alc Margarita — 250ml cans, 7% ABV (rivals sit at 4.5–5%). On the label: "Made with real tequila, lemon & lime juice. No BS."

The price. R169 per 4-pack / R49 a can — the premium end of the shelf against R39–42 rivals, earned with real imported tequila, real juice and higher ABV.

The bottles. Fuego Tequila — Reposado, jalapeño-infused and cocktail serves — already selling nationwide, from the Oranjezicht Market to major retailers. A premium anchor above the cans, fully in the deal.

The positioning. The anti-wine club: competing for share of the cooler box — the braai, the boat, the beach house — aimed at urbanites who already order margaritas out.

04 · The market

Three tailwinds, one open shelf

3rd

SA is the world's third-largest RTD market, volumes +12% in 2024 (IWSR).

~95%

of SA's spirit RTDs are vodka-based (TSB). Tequila RTD is open white space.

2.4×

SA tequila: $129m (2024) → $309m by 2033, 9.2%/yr (IMARC).

10.5%

Global tequila CAGR — $18bn → $45bn by 2033. The fastest-growing major spirit (IMARC).

The momentum is spirit RTDs

Spirit RTDs were the only growing US spirits category in 2025: +16.4% (DISCUS); tequila and canned cocktails were called the year's only bright spots (CNBC). SA's spirit-RTD segment is projected to more than double to ~$2.4bn by 2031 (Mobility Foresights).

The input-cost window

In plain terms: Mexico planted far too much agave, and prices for it have collapsed — down roughly 90% from the peak (France24). Mexican distillers are sitting on more tequila than they can sell (Robb Report). The brand buys its tequila in bulk through the supply agreement, which means its main ingredient is the cheapest it has been in ten years — great for margins, and a rare moment to stock up.

The retail partner: Checkers

SA's fastest-growing grocer five years running — sales +13.8% to a near-R100bn brand, 311 LiquorShops, Sixty60 delivery +47.7% (Shoprite FY2025). With ~660 stores in total, a 360-store exclusive plus Sixty60 covers essentially its entire premium footprint.

What "RTD" means here: the figures follow the IWSR definition — pre-mixed ready-to-drink alcohol (canned cocktails, hard seltzers, long drinks, flavoured alcoholic beverages). Beer and wine are not included. The ~95% figure is an industry estimate of the white-spirit (vodka) share of SA's spirit-based RTDs, reported by The Spirits Business.

One caveat, managed head-on: US tequila dollar sales dipped 4.1% in 2025 as the category traded down — the same softness that created the glut this business buys into. SA's tequila and RTD markets both remain in structural growth.

Why the majors can't just copy this

Supply position

The majors have judged SA tequila importing unfeasible at their scale — existing rivals already buy their bulk from Doña. A new entrant's fastest route to real tequila is to become a customer of the brand's own supplier.

Know-how

Africa's only vacuum distillery, plus years of excise, import and compliance experience behind the supply agreement — capability that money buys slowly.

Position & timing

First-mover premium listing with SA's fastest-growing retailer, a defended price point (no discounting), and input costs locked while Mexican prices sit at decade lows.

And the honest kicker: when a major decides it wants a category, history says it buys the brand that built it rather than building its own — Inverroche→Pernod Ricard, Casamigos→Diageo, BeatBox→AB InBev. That's the exit, not the threat.

06 · Go-to-market

Launch deep, not wide

The listing does the reach

360 Checkers stores plus Sixty60 delivery from August 2026, with a 12-month exclusive. Consumer campaign follows launch. In a listed launch, every store is a billboard — the marketing job is rate of sale, not awareness.

Own one occasion

"Share of cooler box." Target existing margarita drinkers — no money spent converting anyone. Goal: 1,000 repeat customers in the first six months (~2 four-packs/month), found within 100km before going wide.

Partner-led campaign

Premium cooler-bar partnership with in-store giveaways at top stores, surprise cooler-box drops, and a #DoñaFuego photo competition that doubles as audience research. No price discounting — the premium holds.

Checkers is the launchpad — every other retailer is watching

The exclusivity is strategic, not a constraint: 12 months to build the foundation, prove rate of sale and create consumer demand inside SA's fastest-growing retailer. When it lapses, the proven listing story goes to a chain off-trade universe of roughly 2,400 branded liquor doors: TOPS at SPAR (914 stores — Business Day, FY25), the wider Shoprite Group estate (~925 liquor outlets), Pick n Pay Liquor (~459 stores), Liquor City (~300 — Africa Outlook), plus Ultra Liquors, Makro and premium niches — in an off-trade market that passed R100bn in 2024 (Daily Investor), behind which sit ~40,000 licensed taverns and bottle stores. A brand that performs in 360 Checkers stores walks into those conversations with proof, not promises. None of this is in the projections.

09 · The ask & use of funds

Fuel for a fire that's already lit

The capital

One strategic partner into the brand entity at a ~R15m valuation guide. Instrument and cheque size are open — equity, or equity plus a working-capital facility.

The use of funds

Working capital through the Checkers launch — the R1.6m peak need (Aug '26) between upfront costs and 30-day terms. Beyond that: stock cover, the launch campaign, and buying tequila under the supply agreement while prices sit at decade lows.

The partner

Beyond money: retail and on-trade distribution, brand-building capability, RTD/spirits category experience — and appetite for the follow-on (new retailers, export, scaled bulk).

Why now, not next year

  • The price won't wait. The raise is guided at ~R15m on the story and the listing. Once Checkers actuals land, the valuation re-rates on evidence — in either direction. Waiting is not a free option.
  • The exclusivity clock starts in August. The partner who helps convert the 12-month window joins now, not after it closes.
  • The input-cost window is cyclical. Tequila at decade-low prices lasts only until Mexican supply corrects.
  • One partner seat. This is not a round with allocations.

The big picture

The plan is built toward a R500m+ business, in stages that each fund the next. Stage 1: own tequila RTD in the world's third-largest RTD market as SA's spirit-RTD segment grows toward ~$2.4bn. Stage 2: take the brands into export through the distillery's existing European and North American footprint. Stage 3: ride the supply platform as the region's tequila source — earning as the whole category grows. The endgame comps are real: AB InBev paid $490m for BeatBox, Diageo ~$1bn for Casamigos, and Pernod Ricard bought SA's own Inverroche. Stated plainly: R500m+ is the ambition this plan builds toward — an ambition, not a projection.

10 · Investor FAQ

Asked and answered

This list grows as investors ask. If your question isn't here, ask it — it'll help the next reader.

What problem is Doña Fuego actually solving?
Three at once. For drinkers: a real margarita needs a bottle, limes and a bartender — there is no credible grab-and-go serve in SA. For retailers: RTD is the country's second-biggest drinks segment but almost entirely vodka-based — buyers need premium SKUs beyond vodka. For other brands: nobody else imports real tequila at scale — which is why several buy theirs from Doña Distillery.
Is this a product launch or a company?
A company. Doña Distillery has operated since 2019 — the same playbook has launched multiple brands that sell locally and internationally (Mahala Botanical in the UK, Toor Whisky, the Fuego Tequila bottled range), exports to Europe and North America, and supplies bulk tequila to competing RTD brands. Doña Fuego is the newest brand built on that machine.
What exactly am I investing in?
A dedicated brand entity holding the Doña Fuego and Fuego Tequila trademarks, the four-SKU can range, the Checkers listing (360 stores + Sixty60) and future retail relationships — secured by a special long-term supply agreement under which Doña Distillery supplies bulk tequila to the brands on locked-in terms. It excludes the distillery itself: its bulk-trading arm, Mahala Botanical, Toor Whisky and contract distilling. Final structure and terms are agreed in due diligence.
Are the Fuego Tequila bottles included?
Yes. The bottled range — 750ml Reposado, jalapeño-infused tequila and cocktail serves — is part of the brand entity, and is already selling nationwide, from the Oranjezicht Market to major retailers. Bottle revenue is not in the model at all — it comes over and above the projected can numbers.
Isn't Checkers a concentration risk?
It's the launch springboard, not the whole plan. The exclusive runs 12 months from August 2026 — a paid-for national launch with a clock on it — after which multi-retailer, on-trade and export channels open, none of which are in the projections. Meanwhile the Fuego Tequila bottles already sell outside Checkers nationwide, and the supply agreement secures the input side regardless of any single retailer. The projections model Checkers alone at flat reorders — no other retailer, no on-trade, no export.
What stops Diageo, Heineken or AB InBev from copying this?
Three things. Supply: the majors have judged SA tequila importing unfeasible at their scale — existing rivals already buy their bulk from Doña Distillery, the brand's contracted supplier. Know-how: Africa's only vacuum distillery plus years of excise, import and compliance experience. Position: a first-mover premium listing with SA's fastest-growing retailer and input costs locked at decade lows. And if a major wants the category badly enough later — history says they buy the brand that built it (Casamigos, Inverroche, BeatBox). That's the exit, not the threat.
Why invest now rather than wait for the launch numbers?
Because the price won't wait. The raise is guided at ~R15m on the strength of the listing and the story; once trading actuals land, the valuation re-rates on evidence — in either direction. Add the 12-month exclusivity clock already running, a cyclical input-cost window that closes as Mexican supply corrects, and a single partner seat, and waiting a year is the expensive option.
How does this become a R500m+ business, not just a nice brand?
In stages, each funded by the last. Stage 1: own tequila RTD in the world's third-largest RTD market as it grows toward ~$2.4bn. Stage 2: take the brands into export through the distillery's existing European and North American footprint. Stage 3: ride the regional tequila supply platform as the whole category grows. The comparables are real: AB InBev paid $490m for BeatBox, Diageo ~$1bn for Casamigos, and Pernod Ricard bought SA's own Inverroche. R500m+ is the ambition this plan is built toward — stated as ambition, not projection.
Where does the ~R15m valuation come from?
An independent triangulation of three methods on the Brand Pro Forma's 2027 figures: EV/EBITDA (6–12×), EV/Revenue (1–2.5×) and a simplified DCF (WACC 15–22%). The blended brand range is R11.8m–R23.2m with a base of R16.8m; the ~R15m guide deliberately sits below base — before counting the bottled range, post-exclusivity retail or export. Full workings are in the downloadable Pro Forma's Valuation sheet.
What is the money actually used for?
First, the working-capital gap of the Checkers launch — tequila, cans and SARS excise are paid before Checkers' 30-day terms pay out, creating a peak funding need of R1.6m in August 2026. Beyond that: deeper stock cover for reorders, the launch consumer campaign, and buying tequila under the supply agreement while Mexican prices are at decade lows.
Isn't the tequila boom cooling?
In the US, dollar sales dipped 4.1% in 2025 as the category traded down — while volumes held and canned cocktails kept growing. That softness is exactly what crashed agave and bulk-liquid prices, which is a buy-side gift for the brands. South Africa's tequila market (forecast +9.2%/yr to 2033) and RTD market (world's third largest) are both still in structural growth.
What happens when the Checkers exclusivity ends?
The launchpad opens onto the whole market. After the 12 months lapse (late 2027), a proven Checkers listing story goes to roughly 2,400 branded liquor doors — TOPS at SPAR (914 stores), the Shoprite Group estate (~925 liquor outlets), Pick n Pay Liquor (~459), Liquor City (~300) and others — plus on-trade and export. None of that is in the projections, which assume Checkers alone at flat reorders.
Who else is on the SA tequila RTD shelf?
A handful of young brands (see section 05) — and several of them, including Patch and Cheeky, buy their bulk tequila from Doña Distillery. Doña Fuego holds the premium slot: real imported tequila, real juice, 7% ABV versus the field's 4.5–5%.
Why is the can gross margin 30% and not higher?
Because it's honest: it carries the full 20% SARS excise line, packaging inflation and co-pack fees. The supply agreement protects the biggest input — real tequila at bulk terms — and the premium R49 price point is held without discounting.
Where does the market data come from — does "RTD" include beer?
No beer. "RTD" follows the IWSR definition: pre-mixed ready-to-drink alcohol — canned cocktails, hard seltzers, long drinks and flavoured alcoholic beverages. Beer and wine are excluded. SA's #3 world ranking is IWSR 2024 volume data; the ~95% figure is an industry estimate of the white-spirit (vodka) share of SA's spirit-based RTDs, reported by The Spirits Business. Every stat links to its source — see the market section and the source list in the footer.
What are the main risks?
The honest list. First-year retail concentration on Checkers (mitigated by the nationwide bottles, the 12-month clock and the post-exclusivity channel plan). Pre-launch model risk — the projections are assumptions, however conservative (mitigated by the 300-of-360-store basis and flat reorders). Key-person dependency on Danielle (mitigated by a dedicated production manager, co-packed canning, and the partner hire itself). Currency exposure on Mexican imports (partially offset by buying at decade-low prices). We'd rather you read these here than discover them in diligence.
Is there a non-alcoholic angle?
Yes — the fourth SKU is a Non-Alcoholic Margarita, made by a distillery that won a national trophy for Best Low/No-Alcohol Drink and already runs a non-alc brand (Mahala Botanical) sold in the UK. No-and-low is the fastest-growing shelf in the store.
